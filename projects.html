Systems Modeling for Planetary Health

Role: Research Assistant - Duke University, Nicholas School of the Environment

Focus: Climate systems, exposure data, and human health outcomes

Tools: Python, NetCDF, ERA5, NOAA CDR, geospatial visualization, Monte Carlo methods

I support an early-stage research project aimed at building a systems model for planetary health - examining how climate, environmental change, policy, and socioeconomic factors interact over time.

My contributions focus on processing and analyzing 35+ years of climate and exposure data for North Carolina, creating spatial visualizations, and supporting variable selection through exploratory analysis and literature review.

I am currently learning and applying Monte Carlo simulations and Bayesian approaches to assess uncertainty and inform model structure, emphasizing structure before prediction.

QR-Based Dual Pricing System for Small Retail

Role: Project Manager & Developer - North Carolina

Problem: Legal requirement to display both cash and card prices across thousands of items

Outcome: Scalable, low-cost pricing system deployed across three stores

While working as a cashier, I designed and implemented a QR-based dual pricing system to address a compliance and operational challenge faced by a small retail business.

The system includes a centralized price database (CSV-based for accessibility), dynamically generated webpages, QR codes linked to individual items, and staff workflows that allow real-time price updates without reprinting labels.

I led system design, implementation, staff training, and rollout. The solution reduced compliance risk, lowered operating costs, and improved price transparency for customers.
